Chapter 2: Text

Text is the simplest but most important element for communication in multimedia.

Typefaces VS Fonts

It is important to understand the difference between these two terms:

  • Typeface: A family of graphic characters that usually includes many type sizes and styles.
  • Font: A collection of characters of a single size and style belonging to a particular typeface family.

Font Categories

There are two primary categories of fonts used in multimedia:

  1. Serif: Has little "tails" or decorations at the ends of strokes (e.g., Times New Roman). Better for printed body text.
  2. Sans Serif: Does not have tails; it is clean and modern (e.g., Arial). Better for digital screens and headings.

Text Manipulation

Includes Kerning (the spacing between pairs) and Tracking (the uniform spacing between all characters).
