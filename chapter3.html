Chapter 3: Images

Still images are generated by the computer in two ways: Bitmaps (raster) or Vector graphics.

1. Bitmaps (Raster)

A bitmap is a simple information matrix describing the individual dots (pixels) that are the smallest elements of resolution on a computer screen.

  • Made of individual dots or pixels.
  • Bitmaps are used for realistic images like photographs.
  • They lose quality when resized (become "pixelated").

2. Vector Graphics

Vectors are images based on mathematical formulas that draw lines and curves.

  • They can be resized infinitely without losing quality.
  • Vectors require less storage space than high-resolution bitmaps.
  • Requires significantly less storage space than high-resolution bitmaps

Color Depth

The number of bits used to represent the color of a single pixel (e.g., 1-bit for monochrome, 8-bit for 256 colors).
